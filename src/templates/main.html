Some of the worst fires occurred on agricultural concessions

Many big concession-owning companies say they favour sustainable practices and blame smallholders for using slash-and-burn farming to clear the land quickly and cheaply

As fires spread, a choking haze enveloped Indonesia and neighbouring countries

This grounded flights, closed schools and reduced visibility to less than 50 metres in some areas while also taking a terrible toll on public health

The haze contained high levels of harmful pollutants, including carbon monoxide

Some 40 million people were exposed to the smog and more than 500,000 Indonesians sought medical assistance for respiratory illnesses

The fires emitted more CO2 in a few months than some countries produce in a year

Many were in areas where bad land management had dehydrated peat soil, which is carbon-rich and difficult to extinguish when alight
